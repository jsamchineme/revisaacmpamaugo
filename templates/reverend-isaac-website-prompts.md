# Reverend Isaac Mpamaugo — Ministry Website Build Prompts

A personalized version of the WordPress + Claude website template, adapted for a
modern, classy personal ministry site featuring Rev. Isaac & his wife's work and
his sermons and teachings. Use the three prompts below in order.

Before you start, fill in the two bracketed spots:
- `Rev. Mrs. Edith Mpamaugo` — his wife's name.
- `revisaacmpamaugo.online` — the actual WordPress domain you're deploying to.

---

## STEP 1 — Design the website (run this first)

Design a complete 5-page website for a personal ministry using HTML. Make it look
modern, classy, warm, and reverent — elegant rather than flashy. Give it a unique
design that feels timeless and trustworthy, suited to a respected retired minister.

* Business name: The Ministry of Reverend Isaac Mpamaugo
* What I do: A retired Reverend sharing decades of sermons, Bible teachings, and
  spiritual guidance, alongside the lifelong ministry work he and his wife
  Rev. Mrs. Edith Mpamaugo have carried out together.
* Who I serve: Congregants, fellow believers, churches, and seekers who want to be
  encouraged in faith through his sermons, teachings, and testimony.
* Main goal of the site: Showcase his life's ministry and make his sermons and
  teachings easy to find, watch, and share — and let people get in touch or invite
  him to minister.
* SEO keywords: Reverend Isaac Mpamaugo, sermons and teachings, Christian ministry

Build these 6 pages:
* Home — Hero with headline + CTA, trust strip (years in ministry / churches served
  / lives touched), 3 key highlights, sermons preview, a featured "Outreach & Events"
  block showing the 2-3 most recent posts as cards, a short "about Rev. Isaac &
  Rev. Mrs. Edith Mpamaugo" preview, testimonials, final CTA
* About — Their story (his calling and journey, their life and ministry together),
  mission and beliefs, what makes his ministry distinctive, CTA
* Ministry — The areas of his ministry (preaching, teaching, pastoral care, missions,
  marriage & family ministry with his wife), a "how he serves / how to invite him"
  steps section, FAQ, CTA
* Sermons & Teachings — Card grid of sermons and teachings with fitting Unsplash
  images, a filter/topic bar (e.g. Faith, Family, Scripture, Prayer), each card with
  a Watch/Read CTA
* Events & Outreach — A blog/posts page: card grid of outreach and evangelical event
  posts (image, date, category, excerpt, "Read More"), with a category filter bar
  (Outreach, Evangelism, Conferences, Crusades). These are WordPress Posts, not static
  pages. The newest 2-3 also surface on the Home page.
* Contact — Form (Name, Email, Phone, Message), contact info, map, social links

Header: Sticky, logo + nav (Home, About, Ministry, Sermons, Events, Contact) + a "Get in
Touch" CTA button + mobile menu
Footer: Logo, quick links, contact info, socials, copyright

Requirements:
* Modern, clean, classy design — you pick colors, fonts, and style that fit a
  dignified ministry (think warm neutrals, deep accents, elegant serif headings)
* Fully mobile responsive
* All links and buttons work
* Unsplash images that fit the niche (worship, scripture, community, prayer, candles)
* SEO-ready (meta titles, H1s, alt text, semantic HTML)
Build it as one artifact I can preview and export.

## STEP 2 — Import into WordPress with Elementor + XPRO (Claude Desktop only)

Create a new Menu for the pages created for this design. Make sure to also set the
home page.

I want you to take this design and convert it for the Elementor page builder for
WordPress. Make sure to use the native Elementor elements and not HTML widgets. Use
the Container and not Inner Sections. Also I have disabled the atomic editor — do not
use the atomic elements. We have installed the Novamira plugin to help you understand
how to use Elementor elements better.

Use XPRO to design and create the header and footer. This plugin lets you build a
custom header and footer with Elementor. Do not use the Elementor Pro widgets — use
the elements provided by XPRO. Make sure to save the header and footer template in
the XPRO library so I can customize them later.

Use WPForms as the contact form for the Contact page.

## Notes & tips

- "Make the design beat my competitors" was dropped from the original — a ministry
  site reads better as dignified and trustworthy than competitive. Add it back if
  you want.
- For real sermon content, swap the placeholder Unsplash cards for actual sermon
  videos (YouTube/Vimeo embeds) or audio after the import step.
- If you later upgrade to Elementor Pro, you can replace the XPRO step with the
  Theme Builder to get a mega menu and a registration popup.
- Keep his wife's role prominent on the About and Ministry pages — that joint-
  ministry framing is a big part of what makes this site personal.
